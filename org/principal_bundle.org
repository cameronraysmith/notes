* See [[file:Lectures_on_Geometrical_Anatomy_of_Theoretical_Physics_by_Frederic_Schuller_in_2015.org][Lectures on Geometrical Anatomy of Theoretical Physics by Frederic Schuller in 2015]]
** Very roughly speaking, a principal fibre bundle is a bundle whose typical fibre is a Lie group. Principal fibre bundles are so immensely important because they allow us to understand any fibre bundle with fibre $$F$$ on which a Lie group $$G$$ acts. These are then called [[file:associated_bundle.org][associated bundle]], and will be discussed later on.
